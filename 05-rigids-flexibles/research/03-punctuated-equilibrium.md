# Punctuated Equilibrium in Organizations

## Sources
- Romanelli & Tushman, "Organizational Transformation as Punctuated Equilibrium: An Empirical Test," Academy of Management Journal, 1994
- Gersick, "Revolutionary Change Theories: A Multilevel Exploration of the Punctuated Equilibrium Paradigm," 1991
- Eldredge & Gould (original biological theory, 1972)

## Core Concept

Organizations evolve through:
1. **Long periods of stability (equilibrium)**: Basic patterns of activity remain unchanged
2. **Short bursts of revolutionary change (punctuation)**: Fundamental transformation of strategy, structure, and power

## Key Constructs (Tushman & Romanelli)

1. **Deep structures**: Fundamental choices about organizational design, power distribution, and strategy
2. **Equilibrium periods**: Long stretches where deep structure is reinforced and elaborated
3. **Revolutionary periods**: Short, intense periods where deep structure is dismantled and rebuilt

## Empirical Findings (Romanelli & Tushman 1994)

1. A large majority of organizational transformations were accomplished via rapid, discontinuous change across most domains
2. Small incremental changes did NOT accumulate to produce fundamental transformations
3. Major environmental changes and CEO succession influenced transformations

## Triggers for Punctuation

- Technological discontinuities
- Regulatory changes
- Market shifts
- CEO succession (new leadership with different mental models)

## Connection to Rigids vs Flexibles Simulation

The environment in the simulation should follow punctuated equilibrium dynamics:
- Long stable periods where one strategy (A or B) dominates
- Sudden switches to the other state
- During stability: rigid agents with the right strategy thrive and get promoted
- At punctuation: the organization is full of rigid agents with the WRONG strategy
- Adaptation cost = performance drop during transition
- Flexible agents provide "organizational insurance" against regime changes
- The tension: how many flexibles should an organization maintain as a reserve?
