# Beinhocker's Robust Adaptive Strategies

## Source
Eric D. Beinhocker, "Robust Adaptive Strategies," MIT Sloan Management Review, 1999
Eric D. Beinhocker, "The Origin of Wealth," Harvard Business Press, 2006

## Core Framework

Beinhocker applies complexity science to strategy:
- Economy as a complex adaptive system
- Wealth creation = differentiate, select, amplify
- Strategy as a "portfolio of experiments" not a fixed plan

## Fitness Landscape Metaphor

- Organizations occupy positions on a fitness landscape
- High peaks = profitable strategies
- Landscape shifts as environment changes (punctuated equilibrium)
- Companies must constantly explore to find new peaks
- When current peaks collapse, need to have alternatives ready

## Robust Adaptive Strategy Principles

1. **Look in multiple places simultaneously**: Use diverse people/approaches to find higher peaks
2. **Take both incremental steps and longer jumps**: Various time horizons
3. **Don't settle on current peaks**: Continuous exploration
4. **Manage a portfolio of strategies**: Some heavily pursued, some as backup

## Connection to Rigids vs Flexibles

Beinhocker's framework provides the strategic motivation for the simulation:
- Rigid agents = organizations that have settled on one peak (exploit one strategy)
- Flexible agents = organizations that keep exploring
- Punctuated equilibrium = the fitness landscape shifting
- The "portfolio of experiments" approach = maintaining a reserve of flexible agents
- The simulation quantifies the cost-benefit tradeoff of flexibility reserves

## Key Insight

Organizations that appear "optimal" during stability (all rigids, maximum exploitation) are actually fragile. Maintaining some flexible capacity is the "insurance premium" for surviving regime changes. The simulation should demonstrate this quantitatively.
