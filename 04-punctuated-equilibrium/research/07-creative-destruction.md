# Schumpeter's Creative Destruction and Technology Innovation Cascades

## Creative Destruction (Schumpeter, 1942)
- New innovations replace and make obsolete older innovations
- Initial invention less relevant than subsequent diffusion
- Benefits cascade downward and become democratized
- Technology paradigm shifts resemble biological mass extinctions

## Network Effects in Innovation
- Digital markets tip toward winner-take-all outcomes
- Displacement requires innovation overcoming incumbent's network effects
- Critical mass of users needed before positive network effects kick in
- "Hockey stick" adoption curves after threshold crossing

## Parallel to Ecological Model
- Species = technologies/firms
- Fitness = market competitiveness
- Catalytic links = supply chain dependencies, complementary technologies
- Keystone species = platform technologies (their failure cascades through dependents)
- Innovation = new technology entering the web
- Mass extinction = industry disruption/creative destruction wave

## Sources
