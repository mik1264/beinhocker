# Punctuated Equilibrium Theory

## Origin
Proposed by Niles Eldredge and Stephen Jay Gould in 1972. Contrasts with phyletic gradualism.

## Core Concept
- Species show long periods of **stasis** (little morphological change)
- Significant change occurs in **rare, geologically rapid events** of branching speciation (cladogenesis)
- Pattern: stability → sudden dramatic shift → new stability

## Applications Beyond Biology
- **Economics**: Markets show periods of stability punctuated by crashes and restructuring
- **Technology**: Established paradigms persist until disruptive innovations trigger rapid transformation
- **Business**: Organizations exhibit inertia followed by sudden strategic shifts
- **Beinhocker's framework**: Economic evolution follows punctuated equilibrium patterns, with long periods of incremental innovation interrupted by waves of creative destruction

## Key Insight for Simulation
The pattern of stasis interrupted by rapid change emerges naturally from network dynamics when:
1. Systems self-organize into stable configurations
2. Small perturbations are absorbed during stable phases
3. Critical perturbations (keystone removal, innovation) trigger cascading reorganization
