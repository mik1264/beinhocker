# Keystone Species and Cascade Extinctions

## Keystone Species
- Species whose removal causes disproportionate change in ecosystem structure
- Identified by: high motif centrality, betweenness centrality, or link weight analysis
- In Jain-Krishna model: species whose removal produces zero core overlap

## Cascade Mechanics
1. Primary extinction removes keystone species
2. Dependent species lose critical incoming catalytic links
3. Their fitness drops below survival threshold
4. Secondary extinctions cascade through network
5. Process continues until new stable configuration reached

## Simulation Approaches
- **Topological**: Record secondary extinctions during sequential species removal
- **Dynamical**: Simulate population dynamics after removal to find new equilibrium
- **Stochastic**: Use probability-based coextinction models

## Key Findings
- Link weights and indirect effects matter more than simple hub removal
- Nested keystone species complexes exist in real food webs
- Cascade size depends on network topology, not just species count
