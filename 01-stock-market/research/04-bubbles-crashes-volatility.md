# Bubbles, Crashes, and Endogenous Volatility

## SFI ASM Findings
The artificial market shows two distinct behavioral regimes:
1. **Rational regime**: Low trading volume, uncorrelated prices, no technical trading works
2. **Complex regime**: Excess volatility, bubbles, crashes, technical trading profitable

## Endogenous Volatility
- Volatility emerges from agent heterogeneity and strategy switching
- Agents using trend-following (chartist) strategies amplify price movements
- Fundamentalist agents provide mean-reversion but can cause heavier tails
- The interaction between these types creates realistic market dynamics

## Bubble/Crash Mechanism
- When many agents adopt similar trend-following rules, prices deviate from fundamentals
- Positive feedback loops create self-reinforcing bubbles
- Crashes occur when trend-following rules lose fitness and agents switch to fundamental rules
- This creates intermittent dynamics similar to real markets

## Three Market Phases (from related ABMs)
1. Oscillating phase: Regular bubbles and crashes
2. Intermittent phase: Statistics resembling real markets (fat tails, volatility clustering)
3. Stable phase: Near-rational equilibrium

## Key Results from LeBaron et al. (1999)
- Return series show excess kurtosis
- Very little linear autocorrelation in returns
- Persistent volatility (volatility clustering)
- Trading volume correlated with price volatility
- Small amounts of predictability from technical rules and P/D ratios
