# Arthur's Inductive Reasoning and El Farol Bar Problem

## El Farol Bar Problem (Arthur, 1994)
- 100 agents decide weekly whether to attend a bar
- Enjoyable only if < 60 attend
- No deductive solution exists (self-negating paradox)
- Agents maintain multiple predictive models and use the currently best-performing one

## Connection to Stock Market
- Same framework applied to the SFI ASM
- Agents cannot deduce the "correct" model because the market IS the aggregate of all agents' models
- Each agent maintains an "ecology of beliefs" (100 forecasting rules)
- No single rule can remain optimal forever due to emergent dynamics

## Inductive Reasoning Framework
1. Agents form hypotheses (forecasting rules) about future prices
2. They test these hypotheses against market outcomes
3. Successful hypotheses gain fitness; poor ones lose it
4. Periodically, poorly performing hypotheses are replaced via GA
5. This creates a constantly evolving ecosystem of strategies

## Bounded Rationality
- Agents are rational in the economic sense (maximize expected utility)
- But they have limited information and computational ability
- They use heuristics (condition-action rules) rather than solving full optimization
- Heterogeneity arises naturally from different rule sets

## Key Insight
- Deductive rationality fails when the environment includes other reasoning agents
- Inductive reasoning (pattern recognition + hypothesis testing) is the natural alternative
- This creates complexity, not equilibrium
- The market becomes an evolving ecology, not a fixed-point system
